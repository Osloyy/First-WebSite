    Photo of Linus Torvalds

    Linus Torvalds is a software engineer from Finland who developed the Linux kernel, which is a well-known operating system that powers most of the internet. In fact, most websites are hosted on Linux servers, and it's also used for desktop use, but it's most likely not that well known for that. Linus Torvalds started working on the Linux kernel in 1991 as a side project while in college. At first, it wasn't that much until Linus made the project open-sourced, which allowed the same people who used the operating system to work on it and add features to it that they saw fit for their needs or wants. This made it so Linux has many different versions called distros, which are all based on what Linus worked on plus whatever they decide to add to the version of Linux. With that being said, I think Linus is my favorite programmer and software developer in general, and he quite motivates me to work on my own software ideas.
